The problem

Pet care in Singapore is scattered
— and generic AI doesn't know your pet.

  • Anxious, first-time owners get generic chatbot answers that ignore breed, age, conditions and home type.
  • Local reality is fragmented — local rules, the climate, where to buy the right food, which vet is nearby — spread across dozens of sites.
  • It's not just dogs & cats. Rabbits, birds, fish and reptiles are badly underserved.
  • No memory. Nothing connects daily care to advice or to the services you actually need.

The gap

A pet parent asks: “My rabbit is off her food in this heat — what do I do, and who do I call nearby?”

A general model guesses. A Google search returns ten tabs. Neither knows the pet, the city, or the clinic two MRT stops away.

The solution

A profile-aware pet-care agent,
grounded in real Singapore data.

We pre-scraped and embedded Singapore's pet ecosystem into a retrieval store. The assistant answers via RAG over real services, products and reviews — personalised to each pet's profile and neighbourhood.

How it works

An offline data pipeline feeds
a pgvector RAG layer in real time.

① Ingest
Kohepets products + Google Places (vets, groomers, stores, cafés) + reviews
→
② Embed
OpenAI text-embedding-3-small → 1536-dim vectors, HNSW index
→
③ Retrieve
Postgres RPCs: semantic match + geo nearest-neighbour
→
④ Serve
Agent chat, live map & dashboard — all profile-aware

Two stores, one Postgres

Prisma owns app data (pets, care logs); a Prisma-independent pgvector store holds the SG knowledge base.

Idempotent & auditable

Every ingest upserts on a natural key and records a run — safe to re-run, local-first against Docker pgvector.

Decoupled retrieval

A self-contained search layer is the agent's tool surface — semantic + geographic recall in one call.
